Xecuritas: Interactive Crime Analysis & Prevention

Project Xecuritas, completed by a team of four individuals within a three-month timeframe, was devised to address the critical issues of crime analysis, prevention, and protection. The initiative centered on developing an interactive web map experience tailored to showcase crime incidents within Toronto Municipality. This comprehensive platform featured a dynamic heatmap displaying the intensity of auto theft over the years, adjustable via a convenient slider tool. Additionally, users could toggle various map layers, including police division and neighborhood boundaries, alongside the locations of police stations and Toronto Municipality boundaries, providing essential contextual information for in-depth analysis. Detailed insights into auto theft variation within neighborhood and division boundaries, along with comprehensive theft statistics, were readily accessible, empowering users with key insights into crime trends.

Moreover, the platform facilitated seamless data download capabilities, enabling users to retrieve the underlying data for visualization and further analysis. The project was meticulously planned and executed, following a detailed roadmap divided into strategic phases such as data collection, DLL development, webpage layout design, web map integration, testing, and fine-tuning. Leveraging Kanban as a scheduling system ensured transparency, efficiency, and alignment with project objectives. By sourcing data from the Toronto Police Public Safety Data Portal, which included over 55 thousand reported auto theft cases, Project Xecuritas aimed to contribute significantly to societal well-being, fostering collaboration and informed decision-making in crime prevention efforts.
