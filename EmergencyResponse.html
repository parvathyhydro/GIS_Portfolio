Optimizing Emergency Response

:In my project, I undertook an extensive examination of fire service coverage in Toronto using Geographic Information Systems (GIS) methodologies. I began by constructing a network dataset based on the Ontario Road Network data, opting for a simplified approach with a uniform travel speed assumption of 60km/h for fire trucks, without factoring in specific speed limits or other variables. Using this dataset, I generated service area polygons representing 2, 4, and 7-minute response times for fire stations throughout the city, incorporating a standard 90-second delay. Additionally, I assessed the accessibility of public schools and identified regions serviced by multiple fire stations within a 4-minute timeframe. To present my findings, I crafted a visually engaging Story Map using ArcGIS, providing a comprehensive overview of the spatial distribution of fire service coverage across Toronto. You can explore the Story Map I developed for this project at https://arcg.is/nuj8G.
