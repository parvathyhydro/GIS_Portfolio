Evacuation Planning

In this Project , I expanded upon the Train Derailment Evacuation Zone Model to address two critical public safety scenarios. The first scenario involves allocating the population from Zone 1 (Mandatory Immediate Evacuation Area) to the nearest evacuation shelters located in Zone 4 (No Danger zones), providing shelter to individuals who may need to evacuate their homes with minimal warning. The second scenario utilizes Zones 1 and 2 as barriers to non-emergency travel post-accident and during the cleanup phase, impacting bus routes and other transportation networks. Integrating Community Centre, Schools, and Single Line Roads shapefiles with data from the previous project, I created a Shelters Feature Class and a Network Dataset for Network Analysis. Demand points were allocated to the nearest shelter without crossing railway tracks, considering block populations and the restricted network post-accident. Bus routes were reconstructed on the network without barriers, illustrating LTC Bus Routes for London and the routes diverted by Zones 1 and 2. Additionally, a complementary model was built to simulate various evacuation scenarios, aiding emergency planners in planning notices for evacuees, transit commission, and the general public.
