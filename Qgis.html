Maximizing Forest Interior: A Landscape Ecology Analysis of Middlesex County

In this project, I addressed the task of maximizing forest interior in Middlesex County using Q-GIS. Following landscape ecology principles, I utilized a circle analysis and woodlot buffer to identify areas necessitating further protection and remediation. By implementing a 100-meter buffer around all woodlots and conducting circle analyses on woodlots with existing forest interior, I discerned high and medium priority areas for rehabilitation. Utilizing Q-GIS, I created a comprehensive workflow diagram detailing the steps for repeating this analysis. Additionally, I quantified the total areas of high and medium regeneration within the township. Furthermore, I crafted a process infographic illustrating the steps to maximize forest interior, enhancing clarity and visual representation. Throughout the project, data was managed using a GeoPackage format for versatility. This assignment provided valuable hands-on experience in landscape ecology analysis and advanced my proficiency in Q-GIS methodologies.
